What is freight audit software?

Freight audit software is a technology platform that automatically reviews carrier invoices against negotiated rates, contracts, and service agreements to identify billing errors, overcharges, and compliance violations.

For decades, shippers relied on manual review — teams of analysts comparing invoice line items against rate sheets, checking fuel surcharges, verifying accessorial charges, and hunting for discrepancies. It was slow, expensive, and error-prone.

Modern freight audit software eliminates this bottleneck. It ingests invoices from any carrier format (PDF, EDI, XML, scanned documents), extracts every data point using OCR and AI, and cross-references each charge against your contracted rates — all in seconds, not hours.

The result: shippers typically recover 2-5% of their total shipping spend in the first year alone, with ongoing savings that compound as the system learns.

How does automated freight audit work?

Automated freight audit follows a four-step process that mirrors what human analysts do, but at machine speed and scale:

1. Invoice Ingestion

Carriers send invoices in dozens of formats. Automated systems accept PDFs via email, direct API integrations with your TMS or ERP, EDI 210 transactions, and even scanned documents. The best platforms support 50+ carrier formats out of the box.

2. AI-Powered Data Extraction

Machine learning models parse every field: base rate, fuel surcharge, accessorials,重量 (weight), distance, liftgate fees, residential surcharges, and more. Leading platforms achieve 99.7% extraction accuracy — far exceeding human review.

3. Contract Compliance Check

Each extracted charge is cross-referenced against your negotiated rate contracts. The system validates fuel caps, accessorial limits, weight brackets, zone-based pricing, and any custom terms you've agreed to with each carrier.

4. Dispute Filing & Recovery

When overcharges are detected, the platform generates dispute documentation and files claims directly with carriers. It tracks the entire recovery lifecycle — from initial dispute through credit memo issuance — so you get your money back.

Key features to look for in freight audit software

Not all freight audit platforms are created equal. Here are the critical capabilities to evaluate:

OCR and PDF Parsing

Carriers don't standardize their invoice formats. Look for software that handles messy PDFs, multi-page invoices, and scanned documents without manual intervention. AI-powered extraction is essential — traditional OCR alone won't cut it.

Contract Compliance Engine

The platform should let you define your rate contracts in detail: base rates by lane, fuel surcharge caps, accessorial limits, and service-level agreements. Every invoice should be validated against these rules automatically.

Overcharge Detection

Beyond contract compliance, the system should detect common overcharge patterns: duplicate charges, incorrect weight classifications, unauthorized accessorials, and billing for services not rendered.

Auto-Dispute Capability

Filing disputes manually is time-consuming. The best platforms generate carrier-compliant dispute letters, attach supporting evidence, and submit claims through carrier portals or email — all automatically.

Analytics and Reporting

You need visibility into your shipping spend patterns, carrier performance trends, and recovery metrics. Look for dashboards that show real-time audit results and historical savings.

Why AI-powered audit beats manual review

Manual freight audit has three fundamental weaknesses that AI overcomes:

Speed

A skilled analyst can review 30-50 invoices per day. An AI platform processes thousands in the same timeframe. For shippers with high invoice volumes, manual review creates a permanent backlog.

Accuracy

Human fatigue is real. After reviewing hundreds of invoices, error rates climb. AI maintains consistent 99.7% accuracy regardless of volume. It never gets tired, distracted, or misses a line item.

Coverage

Manual teams typically sample 10-20% of invoices due to resource constraints. Automated platforms audit 100% of invoices, catching errors in the 80% that manual review would never touch.
