## Case Study A: Midwest Logistics Cuts Audit Time by 90% and Recovered $47K in Q1

### Customer Profile

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Company Name** | Midwest Logistics Co. |
| **Headquarters** | Indianapolis, IN |
| **Industry** | 3PL — Regional LTL/TL Brokerage |
| **Annual Freight Spend** | $5.2M |
| **Monthly Invoice Volume** | 200 |
| **Number of Carriers** | 30 |
| **Prior Audit Method** | Manual spreadsheet audit by 1 FTE |
| **EDGE77 Plan** | Starter ($0/mo) |

### The Challenge

Midwest Logistics was managing 200 freight invoices per month with a single dedicated auditor. Every invoice was manually checked against emailed rate confirmations and scanned PDF contracts. The process was slow, error-prone, and left money on the table.

- The manual audit required 25+ hours per week, often running 2–3 weeks behind schedule
- An estimated 2–4% of invoices contained billing errors, but only obvious duplicates were caught
- Fuel surcharge discrepancies were almost never identified because contract rates were stored across dozens of separate PDFs
- Dispute submission was handled via email with no structured process — many potential claims were simply abandoned
- The audit backlog meant carriers were paid on time but overcharges were never recovered

### The Solution

Midwest Logistics deployed EDGE77 Starter plan in under one week. Invoices were forwarded to EDGE77's email ingestion address, and the AI engine began extracting line-item data immediately.

The OCR system read every PDF invoice, identifying rate basis, weight, NMFC class, fuel surcharge percentage, and accessorial charges. Each charge was validated against the carrier's contract terms stored in EDGE77's rate table. Discrepancies were flagged within minutes of invoice receipt.

> EDGE77 audited 200 invoices in the first week and flagged $8,300 in potential recoveries — more than Midwest's auditor had caught in the previous three months combined.

### The Results

| Metric | Before EDGE77 | After EDGE77 | Improvement |
|--------|--------------|--------------|-------------|
| Time to Audit | 3–4 weeks | Same-day | ~90% |
| Staff Hours/Week | 25+ | 2–3 | 90% |
| Error Detection Rate | ~1% | 3.5% | 250% |
| Recovery per Quarter | ~$4,000 | $47,000 | 1,075% |
| Dispute Resolution Time | 45 days | 14 days | 69% |

### Financial Impact

- **Total recovered in Q1:** $47,000
- **Contingency paid (15% Starter plan):** $7,050
- **Total subscription cost:** $0
- **Net recovery:** $39,950
- **ROI on EDGE77:** Infinite (no subscription cost, contingency only on recovered funds)

### Customer Quote

> *"Before EDGE77, we had no visibility into how much we were losing to carrier billing errors. Our auditor was doing heroic work, but there's no way a human can catch every rate discrepancy across 30 carriers. EDGE77 found $47,000 in the first quarter — money that would have stayed in the carriers' pockets. The best part? We pay nothing unless they find errors."*
>
> — **Mark T.**, VP of Operations, Midwest Logistics Co.

## Case Study B: National 3PL Recovers $128K in Overcharges Across 12 Carriers

### Customer Profile

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Company Name** | National Logistics, Inc. |
| **Headquarters** | Atlanta, GA |
| **Industry** | 3PL — National TL/FTL Brokerage & Warehousing |
| **Annual Freight Spend** | $32M |
| **Monthly Invoice Volume** | 1,000 |
| **Number of Carriers** | 80 |
| **Prior Audit Method** | Part-time internal audit (2 staff) + periodic third-party audit |
| **EDGE77 Plan** | Growth ($499/mo) |

### The Challenge

National Logistics was processing just over 1,000 invoices per month with a two-person audit team. The team split time between audit work and carrier operations, so invoice review was inconsistent. A third-party auditor was engaged annually for a deep-dive, but by the time errors were found, the carrier dispute window had often closed.

- The two-person team spent 60+ hours per week on manual audit, still leaving 30% of invoices unreviewed
- Contract rate tables existed across 80+ carrier agreements stored as PDFs and scanned documents
- Accessorial charges — detention, lumper, re-weigh — were billed on nearly 40% of invoices but rarely validated against contract caps
- The annual third-party audit cost $18,000 and found $85K in errors, but the lag time meant only $42K was recoverable within dispute windows
- Carrier relationships were strained by slow payment and sporadic dispute practices

### The Solution

National Logistics deployed EDGE77 Growth plan with direct API integration to their TMS. All 1,000 monthly invoices were automatically routed to EDGE77 upon carrier submission.

For each carrier, EDGE77's contract ingestion engine mapped rate tables, fuel surcharge schedules, accessorial fee caps, and discount terms. The AI audited every line item in real time. When a discrepancy was found — whether a 2-cent-per-mile rate variance or a $200 detention overcharge — EDGE77 generated a complete dispute packet with supporting documentation.

> Within 60 days, EDGE77 had audited over 2,000 invoices and flagged $156,000 in billing discrepancies across 12 carriers.

### The Results

| Metric | Before EDGE77 | After EDGE77 | Improvement |
|--------|--------------|--------------|-------------|
| Time to Audit | 3–5 days | 30 minutes | 99% |
| Staff Hours/Week | 60+ | 5 | 92% |
| Invoices Audited | ~70% | 100% | 43% |
| Error Detection Rate | 0.8% | 3.2% | 300% |
| Quarter Recovery | ~$42,000 | $128,000 | 205% |
| Dispute Success Rate | 55% | 92% | 37pp |

### Financial Impact

- **Total recovered (Q1):** $128,000
- **Contingency paid (12% Growth plan):** $15,360
- **Subscription cost:** $1,497 (3 months × $499)
- **Net recovery (3 months):** $111,143
- **Annualized net savings:** $444,572
- **Additional savings:** One full-time staff reallocated from audit to operations

### Customer Quote

> *"We thought we had a handle on our audit process. Then EDGE77 showed us we were missing nearly 75% of carrier billing errors. In three months, they recovered $128,000 — more than our annual third-party audit found in a full year. The fuel surcharge discrepancies alone were eye-opening. This isn't just an audit tool; it's a revenue recovery engine."*
>
> — **Jennifer R.**, Director of Finance, National Logistics, Inc.

## Case Study C: Private Fleet Operator Catches Fuel Surcharge Overbilling Across 45% of Invoices

### Customer Profile

| Field | Content |
|-------|---------|
| **Company Name** | GreenLine Distribution |
| **Headquarters** | Dallas, TX |
| **Industry** | Private Fleet — Food & Beverage Distribution |
| **Annual Freight Spend** | $18.5M |
| **Monthly Invoice Volume** | 600 |
| **Number of Carriers** | 15 (dedicated fleet + overflow carriers) |
| **Prior Audit Method** | Manual review by fleet manager (2 hrs/week — cursory only) |
| **EDGE77 Plan** | Growth ($499/mo) |

### The Challenge

GreenLine Distribution operated a private fleet of 85 trucks supplemented by 15 overflow carriers during peak seasonal periods. The fleet manager was responsible for dispatch, driver management, and maintenance — invoice auditing was a low priority. Carriers submitted fuel surcharge calculations based on DOE/DOT fuel index rates, but the invoices were rarely verified.

- The fleet manager spent approximately 2 hours per week on invoice review — enough to catch duplicate invoices but not nuanced rate errors
- Fuel surcharges were billed on 100% of inbound invoices, using varied base rate assumptions and mileage calculations
- No system existed to validate whether the carrier's fuel surcharge percentage matched EDGE77's contracted formula
- Accessorial charges — detention, stop-off, and re-delivery — were paid as billed without validation
- The company estimated billing errors were "probably happening" but had no data to quantify the impact

### The Solution

GreenLine Distribution deployed EDGE77 Growth plan with Samsara integration. The Samsara connection allowed EDGE77 to cross-reference GPS odometer readings against carrier-mileage billings, validating fuel surcharge distance calculations.

EDGE77's AI extracted fuel surcharge line items from every invoice and compared the billed rate against the contract formula using DOE weekly index data. Rate discrepancies as small as $0.02/mile were flagged automatically.

For the private fleet's overflow carriers, EDGE77 ingested contract PDFs and built rate tables for all 15 carriers within the first week.

> In the first 30 days, EDGE77 identified fuel surcharge overbilling on 45% of invoices — an average overcharge of $47 per invoice.

### The Results

| Metric | Before EDGE77 | After EDGE77 | Improvement |
|--------|--------------|--------------|-------------|
| Audit Coverage | ~5% of invoices | 100% | 20x |
| Fuel Surcharge Errors Identified | 0 | 45% of invoices | — |
| Average Fuel Surcharge Overcharge | Unknown | $47/invoice | — |
| Staff Hours/Week on Audit | 2 | 0.5 | 75% |
| Monthly Recovery | $0 (sporadic) | $12,600 | ∞ |
| Carrier Payment Disputes | None | Automated per error | — |

### Financial Impact

- **Total recovered (first quarter):** $37,800
- **Annualized recovery:** $151,200
- **Contingency paid (12% Growth plan):** $18,144/yr
- **Subscription cost:** $5,988/yr
- **Net annual savings:** $127,068
- **Samsara integration value:** Enabled mileage validation that found $63K/yr in distance-based overcharges alone

### Customer Quote

> *"We had no idea our carriers were overbilling on fuel surcharges. It wasn't malicious — their systems were using different base rates than our contract specified. But the numbers don't lie. EDGE77 found overcharges on nearly half our invoices. The Samsara integration was the game-changer — we could actually prove mileage discrepancies down to the GPS coordinate. We're recovering $12,000 a month that we were just giving away."*
>
> — **Carlos M.**, Fleet Operations Director, GreenLine Distribution
