### Day 14 — Educational: Fuel Surcharges — The Math Nobody Checks
- **Headline:** Your fuel surcharge calculation is probably wrong. Here's why.
- **Body:**
  Fuel surcharges are the most complex line item on a freight invoice — and the most commonly miscalculated.

  Standard calculation: (Current Fuel Price - Base Fuel Price) / MPG = FSC %

  But here's where it goes wrong:

  **Wrong base rate.** Your contract says $3.50/gal base. Carrier uses $3.00. That extra $0.50 cascades into a higher surcharge on every shipment.

  **Wrong MPG.** The standard is 6.0 MPG for truckload. Some carriers use 5.5 or even 5.0 — inflating the surcharge by 10-20%.

  **Weekly vs. spot pricing.** Fuel prices change weekly. Some carriers use a higher week's price rather than the current week.

  **Capped incorrectly.** Your contract caps FSC at 35%. Carrier applies 38%. Small difference, big impact across volume.

  EDGE77 checks every FSC calculation against your contract terms. Not the average — the actual formula.

  We estimate 15-20% of invoices have FSC errors averaging $15-40 each. For a brokerage doing 1,000 shipments/month, that's $15K-40K/month in overcharges.

  Check your FSC calc. It's worth the math.
